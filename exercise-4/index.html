Evaluating Learnability

Affinity Designer vs Figma

Tool Selection & Comparison

I used Affinity Designer for the Business Card, Letterhead, and Envelope. While Adobe Illustrator was a consideration, region restrictions made it inaccessible. Affinity Designer was an attractive alternative, especially since it's now owned by Canva, and they made it free.

Comparing it to my limited experience with Figma, I found Figma's UI slightly more intuitive ("easier to get the hang of"). Affinity Designer feels more like the "industry standard" for robust vector work; it has a steeper learning curve but feels fine once you acclimatize.

Learnability Issues

I encountered two main hurdles that stalled my progress for about 10-15 minutes each.

Issue 1 Time lost: ~15 mins

Changing the Canvas Background Color

It wasn't immediately obvious how to change the document background color compared to tools like Figma where it's usually a top-level setting. I had to search for the solution.

Issue 2 Time lost: ~10 mins

Adding a Custom Font

I needed to use the 'Museo' font for the brand identity. Finding where to import or manage fonts within Affinity wasn't straightforward and required external help (asked Gemini).

Conclusion

To be honest, I did not have any other issues with Affinity Designer besides the two stated above. The application itself was stable and responsive.

The main factor that took up my time was not the tool's learnability, but rather the creative process itself...iterating on designs, choosing colors, and refining shapes. Once I overcame the initial hurdles of the canvas and fonts, the tool didn't get in my way.
