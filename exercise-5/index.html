Design Analysis: Business Card

1. Principle of Repetition

  • Consistent Geometry: I repeated the curved 'hill' shapes and swooping lines on both the front and back of the card. These shapes mimic the rounded geometry of the wifi signal in the logo, creating a unified visual language across the asset.
  • Color Palette: I strictly adhered to the brand's color palette (Lime Green #B3CC1F, Dark Brown #553F2E, and Beige #f7ecdf) across all elements, using the brown for high-contrast text and the green for graphical accents to maintain brand consistency.
  • Typography: I used the Museo typeface for all text elements, including the tagline and contact details, to match the logo's font, ensuring the typography felt cohesive rather than disjointed.

2. Selected Principles & Enhancement

Visual Hierarchy
I applied Visual Hierarchy by using different font weights to distinguish importance. For example, on the back of the card, I made the name 'John Doe' bold and the title 'Manager' lighter; this guides the viewer’s eye to the most critical information first before they scan the contact details.
Contrast
I used Contrast to ensure readability and visual interest. By placing the dark brown text against the light beige background, I ensured the contact information was legible. I also used the bright lime green for the icons and tagline keywords to make them 'pop' against the darker elements without reducing readability.
Balance (Asymmetrical)
I utilized Asymmetrical Balance on the back of the card. By placing the heavy block of text on the left and the large logo and curved negative space on the right, the card feels stable and professional without needing to be perfectly centered.

Design Analysis: Letterhead

1. Principle of Repetition

  • Footer Graphic: For the letterhead, I maintained visual consistency by repeating the exact same footer graphic, the dark brown curve with the lime green 'hills', that I used on the business card.
  • Header Layout: I also repeated the right-aligned layout for the contact information in the header to match the alignment used on the back of the business card.

2. Selected Principles & Enhancement

Alignment
I used the principle of Alignment in the header. By right-aligning the contact details (Phone, Address, Email), I created a strong invisible line on the right side of the page that balances the heavy weight of the logo on the left side, keeping the header organized and professional.
White Space
I prioritized White Space in the center of the document. While the footer is graphically heavy to establish brand identity, I ensured the central body of the page remained clean and uncluttered to maximize legibility and functionality for the actual letter content.

Design Analysis: Envelope

1. Principle of Repetition

  • Curved 'Hill' Motif: For the envelope, I strictly applied the principle of Repetition to unify it with the rest of the corporate identity. I replicated the curved 'hill' motif from the business card footer to anchor the design at the bottom.

2. Selected Principles & Enhancement

Contrast & Grouping
I also applied Contrast and Grouping to the return address by placing it inside a Lime Green rounded container. This ensures the address stands out clearly against the beige background while keeping the text legible in the brand's signature Dark Brown font. The use of soft curves throughout the envelope echoes the geometry of the WiFi signal logo, reinforcing the brand identity.
